The Little Book of Common Sense Investing

By John C. Bogle

Why I recommend this book

Bogle's take on index investing really lines up with how I like to build wealth for the long run and keep risk under control. This book makes the whole market way less intimidating by breaking things down simply, and it's all about keeping costs low. Honestly, after you read this one, you'll probably feel like you can skip all the other investment books—and you won't feel the need to chase single stocks anymore. Sometimes the smartest move is just sticking with disciplined, passive investing for steady growth.

The Intelligent Investor

By Benjamin Graham

Why I recommend this book

Graham's classic advice still holds up, especially when markets get rocky. What I appreciate most is how he keeps things grounded in real value and the idea of having a margin of safety. This book helps cut through all the hype and noise so you can actually make solid, rational choices—whether you're crunching the numbers old-school or experimenting with AI tools. If you want to stay calm and smart in the middle of the market's ups and downs, this is the one you should have on your shelf.
